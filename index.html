About
DECS

A powerhouse of engineering and computer science education — where curiosity meets craft.

The Department of Engineering and Computer Studies stands as a beacon of academic excellence, producing technically-skilled, morally-grounded, and globally-competitive graduates ready to shape the world.

We offer two flagship programs — BS Computer Science and BS Computer Engineering — built for the demands of a fast-evolving digital landscape.

Mission

Provide quality education that equips students with skills, knowledge, and values for professional excellence and lifelong learning.

Vision

A leading department producing globally competitive graduates driving technological innovation and contributing to society.

Excellence

Unwavering commitment to academic rigor, innovation, and the holistic development of every student in the department.

History

Our
Story

The history of the Department of Engineering and Computer Studies (DECS) is a story of growth, transformation, and the enduring spirit of student leadership. Its roots can be traced to the institution's commitment to developing future professionals in technology, computing, and engineering.

Before becoming DECS, the academic unit was known as the School of Engineering and Information Technology Education (SEITE). Under SEITE, programs in computing and information technology continued to develop and respond to the growing demand for technology-focused education. Prior to this, the institution's computing programs were housed under the College of Computer Studies (CCS), which produced its first graduates in 1991, marking an important milestone in the history of computing education within the school.

As the College of Computer Studies expanded, students recognized the need for a unified organization that would represent their interests and strengthen the academic community. In 1995, the Association of Computer Engineering and Computer Science Students (ACCESS) was established — the official student organization representing both Computer Engineering and Computer Science students, promoting leadership, academic excellence, student engagement, and professional growth.

The continued growth of the student population eventually led to the creation of specialized organizations. In 1997, the Association of Computer Engineering Students (ACoES) was formed to focus on the needs and development of Computer Engineering students. Two years later, in 1999, the Council of Computer Science Students (CCSS) was established to serve as the official student organization for Computer Science students. Though they became separate organizations, both ACoES and CCSS inherited the traditions, values, and vision established by ACCESS.

Through the years, these organizations played an important role in student life — organizing academic competitions, seminars, workshops, leadership programs, outreach projects, and community-building activities that enriched the student experience and prepared members for their future careers.

As the institution evolved, SEITE was eventually reorganized into the Department of Engineering and Computer Studies (DECS), bringing together its engineering and computing programs under a unified academic department. This transition reflected the institution's commitment to adapting to the changing landscape of technology and engineering education while preserving the legacy built by previous generations of students and educators.

Following a period during which the Computer Science program was temporarily not offered, 2026 marked the beginning of a new chapter. The return of the program brought with it the revival of the Council of Computer Science Students (CCSS), restoring a student organization whose roots could be traced back to ACCESS. With a new generation of students entering the program, CCSS once again became a platform for leadership, collaboration, and academic growth.

Today, the story of DECS is shaped not only by its academic programs but also by the students who have carried its traditions forward. United by a common heritage and driven by a vision for the future, ACoES and CCSS continue to uphold the legacy of ACCESS while inspiring the next generation of engineers, computer scientists, and technology professionals within DECS.

1991

First CCS Graduates

First graduates produced under the College of Computer Studies (CCS), marking a landmark milestone in computing education at the institution.

1995

ACCESS Established

Formation of the Association of Computer Engineering and Computer Science Students (ACCESS) — the first unified student organization representing all CCS students.

1997

ACoES Founded

Formation of the Association of Computer Engineering Students (ACoES), a specialized organization dedicated to the growth and development of Computer Engineering students.

1999

CCSS Founded

Establishment of the Council of Computer Science Students (CCSS), serving as the dedicated student organization for Computer Science students, building on the legacy of ACCESS.

2000s
– 2020s

SEITE Era

Academic programs continued under the School of Engineering and Information Technology Education (SEITE), strengthening engineering and technology education within the institution.

2026
New Chapter

CS Revival & CCSS Returns

Revival of the Computer Science program and the renewed presence of CCSS, welcoming a new generation of Computer Science students and future graduates.

Present

DECS — Continuing the Legacy

The Department of Engineering and Computer Studies (DECS) continues the legacy of CCS and SEITE, with ACoES and CCSS serving as student organizations dedicated to leadership, academic excellence, innovation, and service.
